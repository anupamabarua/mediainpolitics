What Is Poltical Polarization?

Political polarization is the growing ideology of the division of two political systems. Recently, individuals have aligned with extreme positions rather than moderate viewpoints. In the last 25 years, Democrats and Republicans have moved further apart from each other.

What Is A Echo Chamber?

An Echo Chamber is an environment where your beliefs, ideas, or assumptions are constantly repeated and reinforced. An echo chamber on social media is a digital environment where your existing beliefs are constantly reinforced.

Why Do Echo Chambers Form?

The Algorithm

Social media is designed to make sure we only see media that fits our viewpoints. Regardless of the specific algorithm, social media is used to "connect groups of like-minded users together based on shared content preferences." The result is an echo chamber where users are constantly exposed to information that supports what they already believe. Instead of seeing a variety of perspectives, people are shown more content similar to what they have previously liked, shared, or interacted with. This can make political beliefs stronger and contribute to an increase of political polarization.

Confirmation Bias

Conformation bias is the human tendency to search for and interpret information that supports our preexisting beliefs. You can see confirmation bias when you read “fake news". When we read fake news, we are more likely to agree with information that confirms our beliefs. This is how “fake news” aka misinformation spreads.

Poltical Identity

In recent years, political parties have defined a person's identity. When a person feels like you are challenging their political views, it feels like an attack on who they are.This makes people resist the other political party's information and leads to more political polarization as they dive deeper into the echo chamber they have created for themselves.
