Chapter 06

Political
Cartoons

For centuries, political cartoonists have said with ink and irony what news reporters couldn't. A look at political satire as media and as history.

Why Political Cartoons Matter

Political cartoons are important because they act as a powerful tool for free expression. They turn political events into vivid, accessible, and often funny visual commentary. This allows artists to criticize people of power and hold politicians accountable without censorship. Even with social media, cartoons connect everybody past and present.

Examples

The history of poltical cartoons through the centuries!

1787 · Constitutional Ratification Era

The Looking Glass for 1787

"In this cartoon, both sides tug on a wagon representing Connecticut on the eve of the ratification of the Constitution."

1812 · War of 1812 Era

A Boxing Match

During the War of 1812, the artist portrays James Madison punching King George III in the face.

1870 · Reconstruction Era

Throwing Down the Ladder by Which They Rose

This cartoon shows anti-immigrant Americans throwing down the ladder in an attempt to deny Chinese immigrants entry into the U.S.

1945 · Post-WWII Era

The Blind Leading the Blind

The artist draws Uncle Sam leading a disheveled, bloodstained German out of the war-torn ruins of Europe.

1992 · Modern Era

Welcome to McUSA

By Ted Rall, one of the most prolific cartoonists in American history, this cartoon satirizes the corporatization of American culture.

2017 · Contemporary Era

Lock Him Up

By Andy Marlette, this cartoon critiques Donald Trump's immigration policies by depicting an ICE agent detaining Jesus, who would fit the profile of many immigrants in the U.S. today.
