Information

Information is data or knowledge that is factually correct and verifiable. Good information comes from credible sources and can be cross referenced with multiple sources.

Misinformation

Misinformation is false or inaccurate information spread without the intent to cause harm. Disinformation is false content intentionally created and spread to deceive, manipulate, or harm someone

How Misinformation Spreads

Misinformation spreads in a variety of ways. It is a combination of human psychology, social media, and algorithms. People are more likely to share information that matches their beliefs, identity, or political views. Misinformation is often shared because it aligns with a person's values, not because it's true. Another way misinformation is spread is through social media. Platforms like TikTok and X/Twitter use algorithms that show users content that gets high engagement. This creates echo chambers. People mostly see content that aligns with their views which makes misinformation spread faster. In conclusion, misinformation spreads because it is emotionally appealing, fits people's beliefs, and is amplified by algorithms.
