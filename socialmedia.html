Chapter 04

Social Media
IN Elections

Targeted Ads

Political campaigns use the same tricks that behavioral ads do. Advertisements can persuade voters, increase knowledge, and boost voter turnout.

Influencers

Political campaigns use social media creators to reach audiences. An example of this is Charlie Kirk. Kirk was a right leaning American political Activist. He co-founded the conservative student organization Turning Point USA in 2012 and served as its executive director until his assassination in 2025. He became the voice of the Maga youth.

Viral Content

There are different things that will go viral overnight. Social media has made political content more easy to share, therefore more readily accessible and seen.

Key Moments of Social Media in Elections
2008

Obama's Presidential Campaign

In the mid 2000s, Social Media was created. In 2008, Barack Obama was the first president to use social media to campaign. He used Facebook, Twitter, and email to connect with the voters rather than relying on traditional methods. Through social media, he raised 699 million dollars.

2016

Disinformation

In the 2016 election, the increase of support for Hillary Clinton led to a rise of disinformation that was created to target her. This led to a decrease of media coverage. The Clinton campaign changed their marketing tactics and focused more on conservative values rather than focusing on women rights. Overall, in the 2016 election there was disinformation being shared all around.

2020

The Rise of Tiktok

During the time of the 2020 election, the whole world was in a lock down. This led everybody to use technology more than they used too. The app Tiktok became a place where campaigns, supporters, and influences could read young people. Trump tried to ban Tiktok which led to the center of election politics. Tiktok was important for younger voters because it was a less traditional way to reach them.

2024

Viral

In 2024, TikTok was a major part in the upcoming election. Candidates and campaigns worked more directly with influencers. Campaigns had their own social media accounts like KamalaHQ, which is now Headquarters on TikTok. Kamala Harris used memes and viral clips to engage young voters. Some examples of this are Watch Kamala Harris' viral "coconut tree" remarks and Sisters: Kamala and Mya Harris Share A Great Moment.
