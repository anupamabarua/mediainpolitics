The Modern Campaign Playbook

Today's campaigns run 24/7 across a dozen platforms simultaneously. A candidate might post a policy video on YouTube, share a meme on Instagram, engage critics on X (Twitter), film behind-the-scenes on TikTok, run 50 different targeted Facebook ads at once, and appear on a podcast — all on the same day. Each platform has its own rules, audiences, and styles that campaigns must master.

Ad Breakdown: What Makes This Ad Work?

Read this real-style political ad, then identify the persuasion techniques being used.

SPONSORED · PAID FOR BY AMERICA FIRST PAC
"Our Children Deserve Better"

Sarah is a teacher in Ohio. For 22 years, she's dedicated her life to her students. But under [Opponent's] policies, schools are struggling. Parents can't afford rent. Working families are left behind.

It doesn't have to be this way.

[Candidate] will cut taxes for working families, protect our schools, and restore American values. Because our children deserve a future as great as our past.

I'm [Candidate], and I approve this message.

Identify The Techniques — Click all that apply:
① Emotional storytelling — using a real person's story to create empathy and make abstract policy feel personal
② Pure factual argument — listing statistics and policy details without emotional framing
③ Contrast framing — defining the opponent negatively, then offering the candidate as the solution
④ Nostalgia appeal — "as great as our past" evokes a golden age, implying something was lost
⑤ Policy specificity — providing detailed, verifiable policy positions with budget estimates
⑥ Values language — "American values," "working families," "our children" — broad appeal to shared identity
